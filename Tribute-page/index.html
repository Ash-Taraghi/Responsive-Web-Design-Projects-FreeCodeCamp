An Albrecht Drürer Biogrophy

A painting of the German painter, printmaker, and theorist of the German Renaissance: Albrecht Drürer

Here is a timeline of Albrecht Drürer life
(1471-1528)

Albrecht Drürer Timeline:
  • May 1471: Albrecht Drürer is born in Nuremberg
  • 1486 - 1489: Albrecht Drürer studied as a painter and engraver as an apprentice under Michael Wolgemut
  • 1492 - 1494: The Gernman Renaissance artist works in Basel providing woodcut prints for books
  • 1494: The artist married Agnes Fey
  • 1495 - 1497: Albrecht Drürer makes first trip to Italy
  • 1498: Albrecht Drürer Creates his 15 prints known as the Apocalypse
  • 1500: The artist produced his famous self portrait now in Munich
  • 1504: The artist painted his adoration of the Magi. The Adam and Eve print was made as well.
  • 1505-1507: The artist visits Italy for a second time. The artist buys a house (now museum) in Nuremberg.
  • 1512-1519: The artist worked as a court artist of Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor
  • 1514: The artist produced his Master Engraving collection
  • 1515: The artist supervises the muliple page print, the Triumphal Arch of Maximilian
  • 1520: The artist is appointed the court artist of Charles V, Holy Roman Empire
  • 1521: The artist visits Ghent, Bruges, Antwerp, and Cologne.
  • 1525: The artist writes Underweysung der Messung, a treatise on geometry.
  • 1526: The German Renaissance artist Albrecht Drürer paints his Four Apostles panels
  • 1528: Albrecht Drürer writes his Vier Bücher menschlicher porportion, a treatise on poportion before his death.
